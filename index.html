Introduction

What Is Sentiment Analysis?

Sentiment analysis is a tool that can tell you if customers feel positive, negative, or neutral when contacting your company. It uses technology to analyze the words and tone in customer messages, such as emails, chats, or phone calls.

Imagine you're a manager at a contact center and want to know if customers are happy or upset with your products or services. Instead of reading everything yourself, sentiment analysis can quickly sort messages and tell you if they are positive, negative, or just okay.

About the Project

Aim :

The aim of sentiment analysis is to develop computational methods for accurately determining the emotional tone or opinion expressed in text, such as tweets or product reviews. This enables understanding whether the sentiment conveyed is positive, negative, or neutral, facilitating insights into public opinion, customer feedback, and brand perception.

Abstract

In this study, we delved into the analysis of public opinion through sentiment analysis, leveraging social media platforms like Twitter and Facebook as rich sources of user sentiment. Traditional machine learning models such as decision trees, logistic regression, k-nearest neighbors (KNN), support vector machines (SVM), along with ensemble methods like bagging and boosting, were employed for sentiment analysis. Additionally, we integrated the NLTK (Natural Language Toolkit) library for text processing tasks, enhancing our analysis with advanced natural language processing techniques. Our experiments involved comparing models with and without NLTK preprocessing, aiming to understand its impact on sentiment analysis accuracy. Furthermore, we explored term frequency-inverse document frequency (TF-IDF) and word embedding techniques across multiple datasets. These methodologies allowed for a comprehensive examination of sentiment analysis methods, their efficacy, and insights derived from social media data regarding public opinion.

Model Training and Data Preparation

Preparing the Dataset

For this project, the sentiment analysis dataset was sourced from Kaggle. The dataset contains text data along with corresponding sentiment labels, which are used for training and evaluating sentiment analysis models.

Kaggle Dataset link

Preprocessing the Dataset

The dataset underwent preprocessing steps to clean and prepare the text data for model training. This included removing special characters, URLs, HTML tags, punctuation, and converting text to lowercase. Additionally, categorical variables were encoded, and missing values were handled.
